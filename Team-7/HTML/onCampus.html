Popular Places to Hangout:

Babbio Patio
The Patio is located outside the Front Gate of Babbio Center, where there are benches and a fantastic view.

Gateway North & South
These are two connected buildings with a overbridge across the Sixth Street. There are plenty of rooms for classes in Gateway South and places for students to do selfstudy in Gateway North.

Tents on the Lawn
Several tents are set outside of Humphreys and Palmer Halls. They are a great place to visit if you stayed indoors for too long due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

Samuel C Williams Library
If you want to study somewhere, Samuel C Williams Library would be your best choice. This library is equipped with several private meeting rooms, dozens of tables in different sizes, and hundreds of seats prepared so you can get an A+ in all your subjects.

The Cannon
Luckily, this French Revolutionary War-era cannon cannot fire. It is located near Howe Building, where you enter the entrance by walking straight from the hallway outside the south gate.

Babbio Center
Babbio Center is really beautiful if you enter from the Gate that towards the Hudson River. In advance, this building contains several seats mainly on 2nd and 3rd floor that you can just have a seat and kill time.
This building is located at the southeast side of the crossroads of Sixth Street and River Street.

Popular Food Places:

Pierce Dining Hall
A place where you can spend over 10 USD for a buffet. This place opens from 6 A.M. to 12 A.M. and 7 days in a week. Pierce Dining Hall is located at the 2nd floor of Howe Building.

Pierce Cafe
A place where you can get coffee, snacks, and some limited grill options after the Dining Hall closes. This place opens from 6 A.M. to 12 A.M. and 7 days in a week.
This dining hall is located at the 2nd floor of Howe Building, in front of the entrance to the Pierce Dining Hall
